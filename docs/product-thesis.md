# Modryn Studio — Product Thesis

**Project:** Lawn Agent
**Author:** Luke Hanner
**Date:** April 14, 2026

---

## The Era

We are in the agentic AI era. Software used to be a tool you picked up and put down. Now it acts for you. The product category that wins in this era is not better software — it's outcomes delivered by a pipeline. You don't operate it. You receive the result.

The current frame — user describes the problem, AI hands back the answer — is still reactive. The human initiates. The AI responds. That's not the destination. The destination is proposal/approval UX: the output is already generated, the human's job is to review and approve. Not operator. Not prompter. Reviewer.

This product is the first real-world expression of that principle.

---

## The Problem

Most homeowners who care about their property face two problems at once. They have a knowledge problem — when to dethatch, what fertilizer to use, when the pre-emergent window opens, how to actually get a green yard — and they have to research all of it themselves. Then they have an execution problem — even after figuring out what to do, there's no system holding any of it. No memory. No timing. No follow-through.

The result is the same every spring: start from scratch. Google it again. Maybe ask an AI, input everything manually, get a generic plan, and still have to go buy the stuff yourself.

I felt this in April 2026 in Portage, WI. Time to start on the yard. All the context in my head. No system to hold it. That's the problem.

---

## The Market

The space has activity but no winner. Yard Mastery has 100K users but is a product marketing tool dressed as an app — users know it and say so. A new wave of small builders are shipping now: LawnHQ, Turf/OS, LawnSignal, LawnCraft Tracker. All variations of the same thing — AI chat plus a logging journal plus a schedule. The current best answer for most homeowners is still a spreadsheet or Google Calendar.

None of them are agentic. None of them monitor conditions and act. None of them remember your yard across seasons. None of them propose anything — they wait for you to ask.

The gap is not a better UI or a smarter chatbot. The gap is a system that runs the research, monitors conditions, surfaces a proposal at the right moment, and closes the loop by getting the right product to your door.

---

## The Product

A persistent property brain for homeowners who do it themselves.

It holds your yard profile — zones, soil type, grass type, beds, history, goals — set up once. It monitors weather, soil temps, and season continuously. When a window opens, it doesn't wait for you to ask. It generates a proposal: _your pre-emergent window opens Saturday, here's the product, here's the quantity for your square footage, approve to order._ You tap approve. It logs what was done. Next season it remembers and gets more precise.

This is proposal/approval UX applied to home ownership. The agent does the research. The human reviews the output. That's the design principle, not a feature.

---

## The Build Filter

> Does this require research or analysis that most people can't or won't do themselves — and does the output make their life measurably easier?

Yes. Monitoring soil temps, cross-referencing grass type and hardiness zone, knowing your yard's history, surfacing the right product at the right moment — that's the research most homeowners can't or won't do. The pipeline does it. The human approves it.
